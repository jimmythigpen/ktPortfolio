Katie Thigpen

Musician, Artist, Teacher

About Me

Katie Thigpen, native of Charleston, SC, is a freelance trombonist and educator in the Washington, DC area. She received her Doctor of Musical Arts and Master of Music degrees from the University of Cincinnati, College-Conservatory of Music where she studied with Tim Anderson. She earned her Bachelor of Music degree in music education from the University of South Carolina where she studied with Brad Edwards. Most recently she has studied with Per Brevig, retired principal trombonist of the Metropolitan Opera Orchestra, and Craig Mulcahy of the National Symphony Orchestra.

Teaching

As an educator Dr. Thigpen maintains a studio of private students in the metropolitan DC area. She teaches brass students at the International School of Music, the British International School of Washington, DeMatha Catholic High School, Holy Trinity Episcopal Day School, and with the DC Youth Orchestra Program. She also enjoys teaching once a week at the Forbush School of Prince George’s County as a teaching artist with the non-profit organization, ArtStream. During the summer Dr. Thigpen is on faculty at Blue Lake Fine Arts Camp in Twin Lake, Michigan. Before joining the faculty at Blue Lake she was the director of their middle school camp, Camp Bernstein, and also worked as a camp counselor. Next summer will be her ninth at Blue Lake.

Performing

Dr. Thigpen performs regularly with various chamber groups and orchestras in the DC area. She has performed with the National Philharmonic, Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra, Cincinnati Ballet Orchestra, Dayton Philharmonic Orchestra, West Virginia Symphony, Lancaster Symphony Orchestra, York Symphony Orchestra, Great Noise Ensemble, and the Ars Nova Chamber Orchestra. She has been a featured soloist with the Blue Lake Fine Arts Camp Staff Band in both 2009 and 2010, was awarded a Trombone Fellowship to the Aspen Music Festival in 2011, and won the Eastern Trombone Workshop National Solo Competition in 2013.
